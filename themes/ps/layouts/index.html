Skills-focussed workshops: Get up to speed with skills-focussed workshops

For when you know there's a gap in your team's skills, and you want a workshop focussed around a particular technique

Skills-focussed workshops give you the chance get confident using a specific technique to help solve a problem you have already identified, so you can start using it right away.

Your team gets to practice the technique, avoid common pitfalls, and learns how to operationalise it. It becomes part of your team's arsenal for solving problems in future.

Common activities include: writing effective user stories, lightweight technical documentation with the C4 model and architectural decision records, usability testing (remote and in-person), customer development interviews, user story mapping, and iteratively building personas.

What this looks like: These typically range from a half day to a day long, depending on the topic.

What you get after the workshop: You'll leave with set a concrete next steps for making this new technique part of your process, along with a set of cheatsheets as reminders of what we covered together in future.

Process mapping workshop: Visualise how you work with a process map, then identify pain points and bottlenecks. Learn to fix them together in your team.

For when you're not sure what you need yet, and you want some help thinking through what your process should look like.

You might be starting a new project, or it's been a while since you came up for air on an existing one, and you need the space to see why your current way of working isn't working for your team.

Building a process map together creates a shared understanding in your team of your workflow, and helps you identify the most valuable places to improve - and how best to communicate this to other parts of the organisation you depend on.

What this looks like: This typically is a day-long workshop, for 5-15 people, with a couple of days either side for preparation, and processing outputs from the day.

What you get after the workshop: Within the week of the workshop, I'll share a deck of findings and recommendations you can use, in your own presentations to peers in your organisation, as well as printable, editable high quality versions of the artefacts we create during the day.

Interim Generalist: Fill gaps in your team’s capacity, until you have the talent in-house, or you need a specialist full time.

For when you need to deliver in an area new to your team, and need to hit the ground running.

You might not need someone full-time to fill a gap in your team's skills, or you might have almost made that perfect hire to plug that gap, but still need to deliver something in the meantime.

I can help you deliver against your goals in the short term. I've worked as a product manager, a UX designer, user researcher, and I've been coding Django and Ruby on Rails applications professionally since 2009 and 2011 respectively.

I'm also comfortable building wireframes, service design blueprints, and Wardley maps, and know when it makes sense use them, and when other tools might be more appropriate.

What this looks like: you hire me in 5-day blocks, to join your team for a week at a time, we'll discuss a specific goal to deliver in that time.

I work best in a cross functional team, and you'll typically get the most value pairing me with someone in your team.

This so knowledge doesn't leave the team at the end, and so you can do the same kinds of work without me in future.
