You’ve worked hard to get a product out the door, to your users. Now you want to work in a more agile way, and grow a high performing team.

If this sounds like you, I can help you and your product team be more effective in three ways:

Skills based workshops: Develop new skills, or sharpen existing ones with focussed workshops on a given technique.

Process Workshops: Build a shared picture of what your development process really looks like. Diagnose problems together, identify pain points and bottlenecks. Learn to fix them with your team.

Interim Generalist: Fill gaps in your team’s capacity, until you have the talent in-house, or you know you need a specialist.

Skills-focussed workshops: Get up to speed with skills-focussed workshops

For when you know there's a gap in your team's skills, and you want a workshop focussed around a particular technique

Skills-focussed workshops give you the chance get confident using a specific technique to help solve a problem you have already identified, so you can start using it right away.

Your team gets to practice the technique, avoid common pitfalls, and learns how to operationalise it. It becomes part of your team's arsenal for solving problems in future.

Common activities include: writing effective user stories, lightweight technical documentation with the C4 model and archiectural decision records, usability testing (remote and in-person), customer development interviews, user story mapping, and iteratively building personas.

What this looks like: These typically range from a half day to a day long, depending on the topic and dpeth

Process mapping workshop: Visualise how you work with a process map, then identify pain points and bottlenecks. Learn to fix them together in your team.

For when you're not sure what you need yet, and you want some help thinking through what your process should look like.

You might be starting a new project, or it's been a while since you came up for air on an existing one, and you need the space to see why your current way of working isn't working for your team.

Building a process map together creates a shared understanding in your team of your workflow, and helps you identify the most valuable places to improve - so you can see where waste from work piling up is, and so how best to communicate this to other parts of the organisation you depend on.

These typically is a day-long workshop, for 5-15 people, with a couple of days either side for preparation, and processing outputs from the day to keep momentum after the day.

Interim Generalist: Fill gaps in your team’s capacity, until you have the talent in-house, or you need a specialist full time.

For when you need to deliver in an area new to your team, and need to hit the ground running.

You might not need someone full time to fill a gap in your team's skills, or you might have almost made that perfect hire to plug that gap, but still need to deliver something until they join you.

I can help you deliver against that goal in the short term. I've worked as a product manager, a UX designer, user researcher, and developer.
